# AAA / The Accounting Review — Manuscript Preparation Guidelines

Quick reference for compliance review. Flag violations in each category.

---

## §1 — Document Order

Required order of sections:
1. Title page (title only — no author names for blind review)
2. Abstract (150 words max)
3. **AI Disclosure statement** (required even if AI not used)
4. Introduction
5. Body sections
6. Conclusion
7. References
8. Appendices
9. Tables (each on a separate page)
10. Figures (each on a separate page)

**Flag if:** author names, affiliations, or email addresses appear anywhere in
the submitted manuscript; AI disclosure is missing; sections are out of order.

---

## §2 — Anonymous Review

The submitted manuscript must be fully anonymous.

**Flag if:**
- Author names appear on the title page or anywhere in the text
- Affiliations or email addresses are present
- Acknowledgments identify individuals or institutions
- Self-citations are written in a way that identifies the authors
  (e.g., "In our prior work (Author 20XX)…")

---

## §3 — Formatting (General)

- Times New Roman 12pt, double-spaced, 1-inch margins
- Pages numbered consecutively
- No running headers with author names
- Abstract: 150 words maximum
- Main text (excl. references, tables, figures, appendices): ≤ 12,000 words for
  full papers; ≤ 7,500 for research notes

**Flag if:** page/word limits are exceeded (note: cannot check formatting from
XML alone; flag for manual inspection).

---

## §4 — Numbers

**Spell out** one through ten in running body text.

**Use digits** for:
- Numbers 11 and above
- Numbers preceding a unit of measure (e.g., 5 percent, 3 km)
- Numbers in a series that includes numbers above ten
- Dates, years, page numbers, table/figure numbers
- Statistical values and decimals (e.g., *t* = 2.34, *p* < .05)
- Percentages (see §5)
- Numbers in equations
- Category labels (e.g., Cat#4, Model 1)

**Flag if:** digits 1–10 appear in running prose where none of the digit
exceptions apply (e.g., "top 10 features" → "top ten features",
"8 times" → "eight times").

---

## §5 — Percentages

- **Body text:** spell out as `percent` (e.g., "the model achieved 80 percent accuracy")
- **Tables and figures:** use the symbol `%`
- **Parenthetical statistical results** in body text still use `percent`
  (e.g., "recall of 88.1 percent (88.3 percent)")

**Flag if:** `%` symbol appears in body text paragraphs (outside tables/figures).

---

## §6 — Section Headings

| Level | Format | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | BOLD, ALL CAPS, CENTERED, Roman numeral | `I. INTRODUCTION` |
| 2 | Bold, Title Case, Centered (no number) | `Hypothesis Development` |
| 3 | Bold, Title Case, Flush Left (no number) | `Sample Selection` |
| 4 | Bold, Italic, Title Case, run into paragraph with period | `Data sources.` |

**Flag if:**
- Level-1 headings use Arabic numerals instead of Roman numerals
- Level-2 headings are flush left (should be centered)
- Any heading uses a non-standard format

---

## §7 — Footnotes

- Use Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3…), not Roman numerals or symbols
- Footnotes appear at the bottom of the page (not endnotes)
- Keep footnotes brief; substantive discussion belongs in the text

**Flag if:** footnotes use Roman numerals or asterisks as reference marks;
endnotes are used instead of footnotes.

---

## §8 — In-Text Citations

Format: `(Author Year)` or `Author (Year)`

Rules:
- **No comma** between author name and year: `(Smith 2003)` not `(Smith, 2003)`
- Use `and` not `&` between author names: `(Smith and Jones 2003)`
- Three or more authors: `(Smith et al. 2003)` after first use
- Multiple citations separated by semicolons: `(Smith 2003; Jones 2005)`
- `e.g.,` before citation is acceptable: `(e.g., Smith 2003)`

**Flag if:**
- Comma appears between author name and year: `(Smith, 2003)`
- Ampersand `&` used between author names in a citation

---

## §9 — Reference List

Format for journal articles:
```
Last, F. M., F. M. Last2, and F. M. Last3. YEAR. "Article Title."
  Journal Name Vol (Num): Pages–Pages.
```

Format for books:
```
Last, F. M. YEAR. Book Title. City: Publisher.
```

Format for book chapters:
```
Last, F. M. YEAR. "Chapter Title." In Book Title, edited by F. M. Editor,
  Pages–Pages. City: Publisher.
```

Rules:
- **First author only** uses Last, F. M. format; all subsequent authors use F. M. Last
- Use `and` before the last author (never `&`)
- **Year after authors**, no parentheses: `Smith, J. 2003.` not `Smith, J. (2003).`
- **Initials only** — no full first names
- **Do not abbreviate** journal titles: `The Accounting Review` not `TAR`;
  `Journal of Accounting Research` not `JAR`
- Alphabetical by first author's last name
- Page ranges use en-dash: `193–225` not `193-225`
- Volume and issue: `Vol (Num):` — space before parenthesised issue number

**Flag if:**
- Year appears in parentheses `(2022)` after author list
- `&` used instead of `and` in author list
- All authors are in Last, F. M. format (subsequent authors should be un-inverted)
- Full first names used instead of initials
- Journal title is abbreviated
- Entries not in alphabetical order

---

## §10 — Tables and Figures

- Each table/figure on a separate page at the END of the manuscript
- Do NOT insert "Insert Table X here" callouts in the body text
  (refer to tables in text as "Table 1" or "as shown in Table 1")
- Use `NA` (not `N/A`) for not applicable cells
- Table notes use `Note:` label; source notes use `Source:`
- Variable definitions and notes appear below the table

**Flag if:**
- "Insert Table X here" or similar placement callouts appear in body text
- `N/A` appears in table cells (use `NA`)

---

## §AI — AI Disclosure Statement

**Required by TAR.** Must appear immediately after the abstract (before the
introduction), even if no AI was used.

**If no AI was used:**
> Declaration of Generative AI and AI-Assisted Technologies in the Writing Process:
> The authors declare that no Generative AI and AI-Assisted Technologies have been
> used in the writing process.

**If AI was used:**
> Declaration of Generative AI and AI-Assisted Technologies in the Writing Process:
> During the preparation of this work the authors used [NAME TOOL(S)] in order to
> [REASON]. After using this tool/service, the authors reviewed and edited the
> content as needed and take full responsibility for the content of the publication.

**Note:** Use of AI as a **research tool** (data collection, analysis) does not
require this disclosure — that belongs in the Acknowledgments or Methods section.

**Flag if:** This section is missing entirely.

---

## §11 — Gender-Neutral Language

Use gender-neutral language throughout.

- Use `they/them/their` (singular) or rewrite to avoid gendered pronouns
- Avoid `he`, `she`, `his`, `her`, `him`, `himself`, `herself`
- Avoid `mankind`, `manpower`, `man-hours`

**Flag if:** gendered pronouns or terms appear in the text.

---

## §12 — Equations

- Number equations consecutively: (1), (2), (3)…
- Reference as "equation (1)" or "Equation (1)" (spell out "Equation")
- Variables in equations should be italicised
- Define all variables immediately after the equation in the text
