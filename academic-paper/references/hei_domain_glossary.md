## Accounting Research Terminology

### Financial Reporting & Earnings Quality
| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Accruals | Non-cash components of earnings; difference between net income and operating cash flows | Core concept in earnings quality research |
| Discretionary Accruals | The portion of accruals not explained by normal business activity; estimated via Jones (1991) or modified Jones model | Proxy for earnings management; see Dechow, Sloan & Sweeney (1995) |
| Non-Discretionary Accruals | The portion of accruals driven by fundamental economic conditions | Residual after removing discretionary component |
| Earnings Management | Managerial use of accounting judgment or real activities to alter reported earnings | Two types: accruals-based (AEM) and real activities manipulation (RAM) |
| Real Activities Manipulation (RAM) | Altering operating decisions (sales timing, R&D cuts, overproduction) to hit earnings targets | Roychowdhury (2006) is the foundational paper |
| Earnings Quality | The extent to which reported earnings faithfully reflect underlying economic performance | Operationalized via accruals quality, persistence, predictability, smoothness |
| Restatement | Revision of previously issued financial statements due to error or irregularity | Serious negative signal; studied in relation to internal controls and auditor quality |
| Financial Misstatement | An error or fraud in reported financial figures | Distinguished from restatement by intentionality; AAER database tracks SEC enforcement |
| Accounting Irregularity | Intentional misrepresentation in financial reporting; synonymous with fraud | SEC Accounting and Auditing Enforcement Releases (AAERs) are the primary data source |
| Revenue Recognition | Criteria for when and how revenue is recorded under GAAP/IFRS | ASC 606 / IFRS 15 are the governing standards; frequent manipulation target |
| Big Bath | Deliberately taking large one-time charges to make future earnings appear stronger | Common during CEO transitions |
| Income Smoothing | Reducing earnings variability across periods via accruals | Distinguishable from earnings management by intent |
| Conservative Accounting | Asymmetric timeliness: recognizing losses faster than gains | Basu (1997) asymmetric timeliness measure; Ball & Shivakumar (2005) |
| Conditional Conservatism | Recognizing bad news faster than good news in response to events | Distinguished from unconditional conservatism (biased understatement) |

### Auditing
| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Audit Quality | The probability that an auditor both detects and reports a material misstatement | DeAngelo (1981) foundational definition |
| Audit Opinion | Auditor's formal conclusion on whether financial statements are fairly presented | Unqualified, qualified, adverse, or disclaimer of opinion |
| Going Concern Opinion (GCO) | Auditor's warning that the client may not survive the next 12 months | Studied in relation to bankruptcy prediction and auditor conservatism |
| Internal Control over Financial Reporting (ICFR) | SOX Section 404 requirement; management and auditor must assess internal controls | Material Weakness (MW) and Significant Deficiency (SD) are key disclosures |
| Internal Control Weakness (ICW) | Deficiency in ICFR; material weakness is the most severe form | Doyle, Ge & McVay (2007) foundational paper; linked to restatements and AEM |
| Material Weakness (MW) | Severe ICFR deficiency where a material misstatement could go undetected | Disclosed in 10-K under SOX 302 and 404 |
| Auditor Independence | Freedom from relationships that impair the auditor's objectivity | Threatened by non-audit fees, tenure, and client importance |
| Auditor Tenure | Length of the auditor-client relationship | Long tenure studied as both a threat (familiarity) and benefit (expertise) to audit quality |
| Big 4 Auditors | Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC | Used as proxy for audit quality; compared to non-Big 4 in many archival studies |
| PCAOB | Public Company Accounting Oversight Board | Regulates audits of SEC-registered companies; inspection reports are a data source |
| PCAOB Inspection | Mandatory review of audit firm quality control and specific engagement files | Deficiency findings studied for audit quality implications |
| Comment Letter | SEC Staff correspondence to registrants questioning accounting disclosures; obtained via EDGAR | Growing literature on disclosure enforcement and financial reporting quality |
| SEC Comment Letter | Same as above; formal SEC review of filed financial statements | Data available from EDGAR full-text search; see Dechow et al. (2016) |
| Audit Analytics | Commercial database (Audit Analytics, Inc.) with audit opinions, restatements, ICW, auditor changes, and PCAOB data | Primary data source for auditing research; widely used alongside Compustat |
| Auditor Switching | Change of external auditor; voluntary or forced | Studied for opinion shopping, audit quality, and regulatory pressure |

### Data Sources
| Term / Source | Description | Notes |
|---------------|-----------|-------|
| Compustat | S&P Global financial database of public company fundamentals | Primary source for U.S. financial statement data (annual = Compustat Annual, quarterly = Compustat Quarterly) |
| CRSP | Center for Research in Security Prices; stock returns and prices | Merged with Compustat via CRSP-Compustat Merged (CCM) database using GVKEY-PERMNO link |
| EDGAR | SEC's Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval system | Source for 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy statements, and comment letters |
| Audit Analytics | Database of audit opinions, restatements, PCAOB inspections, ICW disclosures, auditor fees | Essential for auditing research |
| I/B/E/S | Institutional Brokers' Estimate System; analyst earnings forecasts | Used in studies of analyst behavior, earnings management, and forecast accuracy |
| Thomson Reuters / Refinitiv | Ownership (13F), M&A transactions, institutional holdings | Used for governance and ownership studies |
| BoardEx | Director and executive biographical data, network connections | Governance research; board characteristics |
| ISS (Institutional Shareholder Services) | Corporate governance data, voting records, director independence | Governance and executive compensation research |
| FactSet / Bloomberg | Financial data, analyst reports, market data | Less common in academic research; sometimes used for international data |
| SEC EDGAR XBRL | Machine-readable financial data tagged under XBRL | Enables automated data extraction from filings |

### Research Design & Methodology
| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Archival Research | Empirical research using existing financial databases (Compustat, CRSP, EDGAR) | Dominant paradigm in top accounting journals |
| Event Study | Measures abnormal stock returns around a specific event (earnings announcement, restatement) | Standard methodology; requires CRSP data and a return model (Market, FF3, FF4) |
| Difference-in-Differences (DID) | Compares changes over time between treated and control groups | Gold standard identification strategy; requires parallel trends assumption |
| Regression Discontinuity Design (RDD) | Exploits a threshold in treatment assignment for causal identification | Used for regulatory thresholds (e.g., SOX materiality, PCAOB inspection cutoffs) |
| Instrumental Variable (IV) | Variable correlated with treatment but not directly with outcome; used to address endogeneity | Common instruments: Big N auditor in peer firms, analyst coverage |
| Entropy Balancing | Reweighting control firms to match treated firms on observable characteristics | Popular alternative to propensity score matching in accounting research |
| Propensity Score Matching (PSM) | Matches treated and control observations on the probability of treatment | Used to address selection bias; increasingly supplemented by entropy balancing |
| Fixed Effects (FE) | Controls for unobserved time-invariant firm or year-specific factors | Firm FE, Year FE, Industry FE are standard in archival accounting papers |
| Clustered Standard Errors | Standard errors adjusted for within-cluster correlation (e.g., within firm or within year) | Petersen (2009) is the standard reference; double-clustering by firm and year is common |
| Jones Model | Regression model to estimate normal accruals and derive discretionary accruals | Jones (1991); modified Jones (Dechow et al., 1995); performance-matched Jones (Kothari et al., 2005) |
| Fama-MacBeth Regression | Two-step regression: cross-sectional each period, then time-series of coefficients | Controls for cross-sectional correlation; common in asset pricing and accounting-finance intersection |
| Beneish M-Score | Financial ratio-based model to detect earnings manipulation probability | Beneish (1999); useful as control variable or dependent variable proxy |
| Roychowdhury Measures | Cash flow from operations, production costs, and discretionary expenses abnormal levels | Roychowdhury (2006); operationalizes real activities manipulation |

### Regulatory & Standards Environment
| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| GAAP | Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (U.S.) | ASC codification; compare to IFRS for international research |
| IFRS | International Financial Reporting Standards | Adopted in 140+ countries; mandatory adoption events are popular natural experiments |
| SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act, 2002) | Post-Enron legislation requiring CEO/CFO certification, ICFR assessment, and PCAOB oversight | Major natural experiment in accounting literature; SOX Section 302 and 404 most studied |
| Dodd-Frank Act | 2010 financial reform legislation; includes whistleblower provisions and clawback rules | Growing research on whistleblower tips and SEC enforcement |
| SEC Enforcement Action | AAER (Accounting and Auditing Enforcement Release) or litigation release from the SEC | Primary data source for fraud and misstatement research |
| FASB | Financial Accounting Standards Board; sets U.S. GAAP | Standard-setting research examines comment letters and political influence on standards |
| IASB | International Accounting Standards Board; sets IFRS | Research on lobbying, due process, and international standard adoption |
| Regulation Fair Disclosure (Reg FD) | SEC rule prohibiting selective disclosure to analysts | Natural experiment in information asymmetry and analyst research |
| Clawback Provision | Recovery of executive compensation after a restatement | Dodd-Frank mandated; research on deterrence effects |
